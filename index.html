I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in statistics at Columbia University, working under the supervision of Bodhisattva Sen. Previously, I received my PhD in statistics from UC Berkeley and my BS in mathematics from Stanford.

My research focuses on interactions between probability, statistics, and geometry. Some specific topics I'm interested in include: clustering, dimensionality reduction, empirical Bayes, Fréchet means, optimal transport, Riemannian optimization, and stochastic processes.

Selected publications:

  • Asymptotic theory of geometric and adaptive \(k\)-means clustering. Annals of Statistics. 53(4): 1559-1586.
  • Limit theorems for Fréchet mean sets with Steve N. Evans. Bernoulli. 30(1), 419-447.
  • A strong duality principle for equivalence couplings and total variation. Electronic Journal of Probability. 28, 1-33
