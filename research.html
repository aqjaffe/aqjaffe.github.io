The following is a list of some of the major research projects I've completed and some projects that I'm currently working on.

Vietoris-Rips Complexes of Regular Polygons

Using basic tools of topological data analysis, one can easily compute the persistent homology of finite point clouds. However, extremely little is known about the persistent homology of spaces with infinitely many points. In this project, we were able to compute the persistent homology for the regular polygons \(P_n\) in the plane.

With Henry Adams, Samir Chowdhury, and Bonginkosi Sibanda. Completed as a project from my Summer@ICERM 2017. arxiv slides poster website

Comparing Object Correlation Metrics for Effective Space Traffic Management

Operators in space typically have a priori knowledge of the locations of many satellites and would like to take (noisy) measurements to update these estimates in an optimal way. However, it is generally not known which measurements come from which satellites, so one must employ nontrivial correlation methods to make such associations. We contributed to this field by developing a robust, flexible simulation framework to design, run, and analyze tests for comparing many existing correlation methods.

With Theodore Faust, Marvin Pena, and Julie Zhang. Advised by The Aerospace Corporation. Completed at IPAM RIPS 2018. slides coming soon.

Equidistribution in Binary Necklaces

Consider a discretization of the circle into \(m\) slots and \(n < m\) magnets that are allowed to move freely among these positions. We prove that such a system uniquely minimizes its energy in a state that is given by the unique Christoffel word of type \((m,n)\) and then provide a linear-time algorithm for checking whether such a condition is met.

An indepdent project of mine, initiated by conversations with some fellow students at ICERM in 2017. Check out some current progress in this document.

On Brunet-Derrida Particle Systems

For my undergraduate honors thesis, I will be been studying something generally in the topic of the stability properties of branching brownian motion processes. So far, I've been reading Oksendal's book on stochastic differential equations, this paper by Berard and Gouere, and this paper by Berestycki and Zhao. I expect to start more serious work on the topic in Fall 2018. Advised by Lenya Ryzhik at Stanford.
